✔ TRUST & TRANSPARENCY

How we work, and why you can trust it

Editorial Standards · Version 1.0 · June 26, 2026

Ravenmails covers science, important issues, and fact-checks — accurately and fairly. These are the standards behind every story we publish.

Accuracy & verification

Every factual claim is traceable to a credible source. We verify each story against at least two independent reputable sources, and prefer a primary source — the journal paper, the official release, or the awarding body. We rely on outlets such as the Philippine News Agency, Philippine Daily Inquirer, The Manila Times, Rappler, and GMA News, peer-reviewed journals, and official institutional sources. Single social-media posts are treated as leads to verify, never as sources.

How we fact-check

When we check a viral claim, we state the claim plainly, trace it to its origin, weigh it against primary evidence and expert sources, and give a clear verdict — true, false, misleading, or unproven — with the reasoning shown so you can judge for yourself. We link everything, distinguish fact from opinion, and update the verdict if better evidence emerges.

Crediting people & sources

We name the people behind the work and their institution, link the original source, and never present a discovery or claim as our own. If we feature your work and you'd like to add a quote, change a credit, or request a correction, message us.

Citations

Every article ends with a References list in APA (7th edition) style, so any reader can check our work and go straight to the original research or report.

Photo & image disclaimers

Every image carries a disclaimer. Photos of scientists or events are credited to their owners and used for non-commercial, educational, and news-reporting purposes; rights remain with the owner, and we'll re-credit or remove on request. Any illustration or cartoon marked as such is an AI-generated artistic representation — not a real photograph, and never an exact likeness.

AI-use transparency

We use AI tools to help with research, drafting, and creating illustrations or cartoons. We are open about it: every article carries an AI-use disclosure. Crucially, all facts are verified against cited sources and reviewed by the Ravenmails editorial team before anything is published, and Ravenmails takes full editorial responsibility for its content.

The Ravenmails Creed

  • Verify before we publish — at least two reputable sources.
  • Cite every source, in APA.
  • Give fact-checks a clear verdict and show the reasoning.
  • Always credit the people behind the work.
  • Disclose AI use, label AI illustrations, and correct in the open.
