Six Filipino Minds the World Just Recognized

By the Ravenmails Team · June 26, 2026 · 4 min read

When a magazine sits down to name the 100 people moving science forward across all of Asia and the Pacific, the competition is the entire continent — Nobel laureates included. This year, six of those names are Filipino.

Asia Scientist Magazine has released its Asia Scientist 100 for 2026, the eighth edition of an annual list that, since 2016, has honored researchers, academics, innovators, and industry leaders who won major awards or made significant discoveries in the past year. Sharing the 2026 list with Filipinos are Nobel Prize laureates Susumu Kitagawa of Kyoto University and Shimon Sakaguchi of the University of Osaka. That is the company our scientists are keeping.

Here are the six Filipinos — and why each one earned the honor.

Romulo G. Davide · University of the Philippines Los Baños
Known as the "Father of Philippine Nematology," Davide spent a career studying the microscopic roundworms that quietly destroy farmers' crops. He was named the Philippines' 44th National Scientist in 2024. But his most human achievement may be the Farmer-Scientist Training Program he founded, which has trained roughly 70,000 Filipino farmers to use science on their own land.
Marcela M. Navasero · University of the Philippines Los Baños
A recipient of the Presidential Lingkod Bayan Award, Navasero is recognized for her leadership in natural pest control and agricultural biosecurity. Through her work with the National Crop Protection Center's Quick Response Team, she has helped build cost-effective pest-management systems that protect the country's food supply.
Mary Donnabelle L. Balela · University of the Philippines Diliman
Balela developed modular Kapok booms for cleaning up marine oil spills, earning the Outstanding Research and Development Award at the 2024 DOST National Science and Technology Week. Her booms use biodegradable, locally sourced Kapok fibers that absorb several times their weight in oil and can be reused.
Aurelio A. De Los Reyes V · University of the Philippines Diliman
A mathematician working at the border of numbers and medicine, De Los Reyes was honored for his mathematical modeling of optimal contraception dosing strategies to improve reproductive health. He received a 2024 NSTW Outstanding R&D Award and was named the 2025 National Research Council of the Philippines Achievement Awardee for Mathematical Sciences.
Garry A. Benico · Central Luzon State University
An expert in aquatic biosciences, Benico studies the taxonomy and diversity of harmful microalgae — the organisms behind the "red tide" warnings that can shut down a fishing town's livelihood overnight. Named a 2025 Outstanding Young Scientist by the National Academy of Science and Technology, he is helping strengthen the country's red tide monitoring.
May Anne E. Mata · University of the Philippines Mindanao
Also a 2025 Outstanding Young Scientist, Mata works in infectious disease modeling, applied mathematics, and mathematical biology — using equations to predict how diseases spread and how best to stop them.

Three of these scientists work in agriculture and the sea, two work in mathematics, and one in the microscopic world of microalgae — a span that captures how wide Filipino science really reaches. They come from Los Baños, Diliman, Central Luzon, and Mindanao, a reminder that excellence here is not concentrated in one campus or one island.

To Dr. Davide, Dr. Navasero, Dr. Balela, Dr. De Los Reyes, Dr. Benico, and Dr. Mata: thank you. The world noticed. We want every Filipino to notice too.
